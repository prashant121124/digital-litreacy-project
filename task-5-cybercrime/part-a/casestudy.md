Case Study (200–250 words)

UPI/Online Payment Fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting students in India. It involves tricking users into revealing sensitive information or authorizing fraudulent transactions.

In a typical scenario, a college student receives a phone call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or a seller from an online marketplace. The fraudster claims that the student needs to verify their account or receive a payment. They then send a fake payment request through a UPI app and instruct the student to “approve” it to receive money. In some cases, they ask for OTPs or UPI PINs.

The fraud usually happens in the following steps: first, the scammer gains the victim’s trust through convincing communication. Second, they create urgency or confusion. Third, they send a payment request or phishing link. Finally, when the victim approves the request or shares sensitive details, money is deducted from their account.

Students and young adults are common targets because they frequently use digital payment apps and may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and potential misuse of personal information.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment platforms.
